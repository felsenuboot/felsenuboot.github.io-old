Scenario

BMW wants to increase its market share for cars among the population in Thailand. Suppose that the main competitors for cars in Thailand are Toyota, Daewoo and Nissan and that the total size of the market in Thailand is estimated at 1.000.000 customers.

To figure out the current competitive position, and to help set strategy, BMW has commissioned a customer equity study of the car sector. This began with focus group interviews with clients to figure out the drivers of Value Equity, Brand Equity and Relationship Equity. Based on the focus groups, BMW came up with the following customer equity (sub-)drivers:

  • Drivers of Value equity
    • Quality of the car ("qualphone")
    • Price of the car ("price")
  • Drivers of Brand equity
    • Attitude towards the brand (“attbrand”)
    • Awareness of the brand’s marketing communications (“attad”)
  • Drivers of Relationship Equity
    • Perceptions of the support network (“supperc”)
    • Feeling of belongingness to the brand community (“brandcommun”)

Following the identification of customer equity drivers, BMW hired a professional after sales service to collect survey data from a sample of the population of Thailand. These data were used to analyze BMW’s current competitive position, estimate BMW’s current customer equity, and provide the basis for what-if analyses of potential marketing actions. We assume that each client buys a new smartphone every year (the residents of Thailand like new cars). The average price of a BMW corresponds to an amount of 30 000 euro.

BMW assumes that the average net contribution margin is about 20% (best guess, based on historic company data). Now, a discount rate of 5% per year is a reasonable choice.

Question 5.1

Suppose BMW could increase its customer ratings on price by an average of .35, with an up-front expenditure of 70.000.000 euro, would this marketing investment pay off? What would be the change in customer equity? What would be the ROI? Note: Mention all steps and calculations that you need to perform in order to arrive at your final answer on ROI, fully elaborated (in detail) please.

The ROI can be calculated after the knew Customer Equity is calculated. This is simply done by repeating all steps until Question 4, except with the new Performance Value for the Price.

The CEnew is calculated by using the new CLVBMW and POPBMW that are received, when using the new Performance Value for Price for BMW: \begin{align} CE_{new} &= mean_{i}(CLV_{newBMW}) * POP \\[10pt] &= 12.963,47€ * 548986 \\[10pt] &= 7.116.761.473,96€ \end{align} With the new and the old CE the difference can be calculated now: \begin{align} \Delta CE &= CE_{new} - CE_{old} \\[10pt] &= 7.116.761.473,96€ - 6.389.587.917,06€\\[10pt] &= 727.173.556,80€ \end{align} The ROI is calculated as follows: \begin{align} ROI &= \frac{\Delta CE - Investment}{Investment} \\[10pt] & = \frac{727.173.556,80-700.000.000}{700.000.000} \\[10pt] & \approx 3,88\% \end{align}

Question 5.2

What change in BMW’s performance on relationship equity driver ‘Perceptions of support network’ (supperc) is needed to achieve a ‘break-even’ when investing 4.000.000 euro to improve the BMW’s performance on this driver? In other words, what should BMW’s performance score on synperc be, to result in a customer equity increase of 4.000.000 euro to make up for beforementioned expenditure? Demonstrate your calculations and explain your answer. Note: some trial and error are advised to answer this question, starting from Exhibit A. You may use an excel sheet if this facilitates your calculations. Strictly algebraic answers are also accepted.

The desired minimal new Supperc values were calculated by trial and error: Trying a random number for the difference of Supperc and looking at the resulting customer equity. If the difference between old and new customer equity was higher than the investment, we tried a smaller value, if the difference in customer equity was lower than the investment, we tried a higher value. Like this, we were able to approximate the minimum difference in Supperc, that has to be achieved to break even with the respective Investment.
